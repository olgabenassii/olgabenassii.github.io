Conclusions

Our project successfully explored the ArCO ontology for Italian cultural heritage. We achieved our goal of enriching our understanding of the ontology by developing and executing SPARQL queries.
Despite facing challenges in data accuracy and model outputs, we overcame these through fact-checking and using different LLMs and comparing their outputs. This project not only enhanced our technical skills but also provided
valuable insights into how we can use ontologies to browse our cultural heritage.


However, things can be improved in the future! Starting with:

    Enriching the ontology to include more diverse cultural elements, eg. extend the proposed triples (time period, materials used) to all elements inside ArCo;

    Introducing a more user-friendly system with a search box that doesn’t rely only on queries (like Polifonia’s).
